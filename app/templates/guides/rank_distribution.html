What Each Rank Means

Iron (4%)

New players or those still learning basic mechanics. Focus on crosshair placement, learning agent abilities, and understanding map callouts. Don't worry about losing — everyone starts here.

Bronze (10%)

You understand the basics but need to improve aim consistency and game sense. Practice counter-strafing and learn 2-3 agents well instead of playing everything.

Silver (16%)

Average player. You know how to play but need to refine fundamentals. Focus on: crosshair placement, economy management, using utility before peeking, and checking corners.

Gold (20%)

The most populated rank. You have decent mechanics but need better game sense. Work on: reading the minimap, rotating faster, playing for trades, and post-plant positions.

Platinum (18%)

Above average. You understand most concepts but need consistency. Focus on: aim training (15 min daily), learning lineups, understanding team compositions, and VOD reviewing your losses.

Diamond (14%)

High level player. Mechanics are good, game sense is developing. To push further: master utility usage, learn opponent tendencies, improve communication, and play with a consistent team.

Ascendant (10%)

Top tier. You need to play near-perfectly. Focus on: team coordination, anti-strats, understanding round timings, and maintaining mental composure during long sessions.

Immortal (6%)

Elite players. Every mistake costs rounds. At this level, it's about consistency, adaptation, and minimizing deaths. Many pro players sit in Immortal when not scrimming.

Radiant (0.4%)

Top 500 per region. The absolute best. These are pro players, streamers, and aspiring pros. Only ~500 players in each region hold Radiant at any time.
