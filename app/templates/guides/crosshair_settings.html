Best Crosshair Settings 2026

Pro player crosshair settings and how to find your perfect crosshair for Valorant.

What Makes a Good Crosshair?

A good crosshair should be:

  • Visible — you need to see it on all backgrounds (light and dark maps)
  • Small — large crosshairs obscure enemies at range
  • Non-distracting — avoid movement error and firing error indicators (turn them off)
  • Consistent — don't change it every week, let your muscle memory adapt

Recommended Settings

Beginner-Friendly Crosshair

Color: Cyan or Green (high visibility). Inner lines: Length 4, Thickness 2, Offset 2. Outline: On (1). No center dot.

Code: 0;P;c;5;h;0;0l;4;0o;2;0a;1;0f;0;1b;0

This is what most pros use with minor variations. Simple, clean, works everywhere.

Dot-Only Crosshair

Some players prefer just a center dot. Good for AWP/Operator players. Color: White. Dot size: 2-3.

Code: 0;P;c;5;h;0;d;1;sz;2;0l;0;0a;0;1b;0

What to Turn OFF

  • Movement Error — shows inaccuracy when moving. Distracting.
  • Firing Error — crosshair expands when shooting. Distracting.
  • Show Spectated Player's Crosshair — turn off to keep consistency.

How to Import a Crosshair Code

  1. Open Valorant Settings
  2. Go to Crosshair tab
  3. Click "Import Profile Code" (top right)
  4. Paste the code and click Import
